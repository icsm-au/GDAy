Definition of the Geocentric Datum of Australia (GDA)

As proclaimed in the Australian Commonwealth Gazette of 6 September 1995, the Geocentric Datum of Australia is defined by the following parameters:

Reference ellipsoid
  • Geodetic Reference System 1980 (GRS80): a reference ellipsoid that has its centre coincident with the earth’s centre of mass, thus producing a best fit for the entire globe.
  • Fundamental parameters:
    Semi-major axis (a):  6,378,137.0 metres
    Semi-minor axis (b):  6,356,752.314 metres
    Flattening (f):  1/298.257222101
    • Reference frame
  • International Terrestrial Reference Frame 1992 (ITRF92): a global network of accurate coordinates (and their velocities) maintained by the International Earth Rotation Service (IERS). The ITRF is derived from satellite based technologies, and therefore has its geometric origin coincident with the earth’s centre of mass.

    Coordinate sets produced on the GDA

    Geocentric Datum of Australia 1994 (GDA94)
  • The fundamental geographic coordinate set derived from the first-order adjustment of coordinates tied to the ITRF92 at epoch 1994.
    • Map Grid of Australia 1994 (MGA94)
  • The resultant grid, or projection coordinates , derived from a Universal Transverse Mercator projection of the GDA94 coordinate set, using the GRS80.
