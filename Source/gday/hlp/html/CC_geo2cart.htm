Geographic to cartesian (spheroid to earth centred XYZ) formula

The formula used by GDAy to convert geographic coordinates of latitude, longitude and ellipsoidal height (f, l, h) to three dimensional, earth-centred cartesian coordinates of X, Y and Z is Bowring's formula (Bowring, 1985).

Notes

  • For more information, see the topic Conversion between Geographical and Cartesian Coordinates in the GDA Technical Manual(http://www.anzlic.org.au/icsm/gdatm/).
  • Although this method of coordinate transformation requires a valid ellipsoidal height, you are not prompted to enter in any such value when using GDAy. Where this cocomputation is performed, an ellipsoidal height of zero (0) is used by default.
